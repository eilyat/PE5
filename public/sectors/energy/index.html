Assessment of investment in an industry

What is the flow-on impact of an investment to the economy?

We provide expert advice on areas of energy sector. Principal economics are experts in the field of energy economics with experience in developing and adopting economic models assessing energy issues in New Zealand.

We provide you a robust evidence-based framework for addressing energy related issues using the Principal Economics’ Computable General Equilibrium (CGE) model of the New Zealand and regional economies.

Our CGE model disaggregates power generation by fuel type (hydro, gas, geothermal, wind, other renewables), allowing us to answer your questions on how new policies or investments (including those related to climate change related programs) impact the energy and electricity sector.

Our econometrics and simulation models also provide a robust forecast tool for energy and electricity demand and prices. Our recent projects include providing forecasts for peak and off-peak electricity demand to our clients.
